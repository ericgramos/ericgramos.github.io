Eric Ramos (He/Him)

Beginning in the Fall of 2023, I will be a tenure track assistant professor at the Stevens Institute of Technology. Previously, I was an NSF Postdoctoral Scholar at the University of Michigan, as well as a Paul Olum Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Oregon , and an assistant professor at Bowdoin College. I am currently supported by the NSF grant DMS-2137628. In the past, I was supported by NSF Grant DMS-1704811.

Math

  • My primary research focus is in combinatorics and its interactions with representation theory, topology, and algebraic geometry. I am particularly interested in using the representation theories of certain combinatorially flavored categories to exhibit and explain certain stability phenomena throughout topology, algebraic geometry, and other fields. More recently I have also been incorporating a number of computational and quantitative methods into my work.

  • My previous postdoctoral mentors were Andrew Snowden and Nicholas Proudfoot

  • My Ph.D. advisor was Jordan Ellenberg
